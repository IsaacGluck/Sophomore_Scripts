Suzuki however, did not last for ever and when I was 12 I moved on to private lessons with private teachers where I had more freedom to experiment and grow as a violinist.

Here I played on some of the best premiere teams in the state. Last year however (2011), life threw me a curve ball as I injured my back extremely seriously. Whether or not I will need surgery is still unkown but I have not been active for over a year and I will likely never play at the premiere level again. The initial effect of this loss was devestating however, I quickly realized that I was never going to play proffesionaly, I had to stop at some point. This tragedy also lead to a large free up of my time which allowed me to focus on other important aspects of my life like music and school.
